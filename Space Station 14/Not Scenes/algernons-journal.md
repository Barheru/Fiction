### November 14, 2525

I have finally met this "Aimee Ungoliant". She is, unfortunately for me, absolutely lovely. This was to be expected, of course, but I had still had my hopes that she would be plain and thus easier to resist.

As it is--there is a part of me that truly could settle for her. In terms of looks, it wouldn't be settling at all--but of course, it's never really been about looks for me. Not... not in the end.

More unfortunately, she seems sly. While she did initially let it slip that she has no desire to pursue this marriage, she has also been most tactful with her words. I fear I may, in my surprise, have pressed too hard with too little preparation.

I can use this. I can use *her*. If I can convince her to make some move against House Ungoliant--even if it is just to try to separate from them--I can take that information to HER Matron and exchange it for my own protection. 

This will have to be done carefully, of course. I will need solid evidence that Aimee is plotting against her House in order to secure my own ends. She may even need to be baited or encouraged into such treachery.

But if I succeed-- then I am free.


Adendum:
After some careful positioning, I was able to get her to reveal slightly more to me. Firstly, there may be other suitors. This is dangerous to my plan, but hopefully, there is no reason for concern... yet. 

Second, she can most certainly be maneuvered. While she is understandably cautious, she said that in a few days' time--when she can tell that I haven't told my Matron or hers what she revealed to me about her distaste for this betrothal--she will be ready to discuss potential ways for us to both get out of this situation. This reveals a naivete that I did not expect from her, but I can certainly use. There is no way out of the Houses. But that is why my plan is simply to be taken in to hers for my services, and not to try to foolishly escape.

I do not need to escape Arach, as she feels she does. As long as my heart is my own, and I am provided for in luxury--this is enough. 

It is a shame that I will have to make such kind promises to her, but such is the way of a spy. I have made and broken such promises before.



### November 17, 2525

I returned to station once again. I could go into the details of my thoughts regarding my plans, but such things are now irrelevant.

She is-- she--
I scarcely know what to write.

She is cleverer than I had perhaps thought. I knew that she had some slyness to her--a daughter of House Ungoliant would, of course, have some natural cleverness to her.

But she actually made a move.
She made a move of her own.
She drugged me. Not much, but enough to remind me not to get comfortable. She was all charm until I felt it hit my chest. She's clever. She's crafty. She's--

It was an aphrodisiac. It's just the aphrodisiac.
I already considered her lovely, this is nothing new.
Nothing has changed. She--she's just a lovely, clever person who has gained more of my respect through this maneuver and--

I need to calm myself.
I can use this. If I find her so charming, then, well, I can lean into this. I can use this. I do need to convince her to trust me, after all--so what if some of it is real? This changes nothing, and, in fact, perhaps even aids my endeavors.

She has won my respect today. That is all.


### November 24, 2525

I finally had a more sincere conversation with Aimee. 

She is, it seems, not fully set on betraying her house yet. I will have to continue to nudge her in this direction. However, she did provide valuable information today. Some person—someone who uses the name “D Edmunds”—has offered to help her “disappear.” Whether or not one can actually successfully hide from the Great Houses is immaterial. My course is clear: I must find this person and either extract condemning information from them, or, perhaps, posing as them, I must manipulate her into giving me such information. This should not be terribly difficult to do.

She is so kind, it seems. She genuinely wants to “help” me. This will be her downfall. She is clever, yes, she is even willing to make such dramatic moves as she did when she drugged me—but in the end, she is a good person.

The world is not kind to good people. 


### December 6, 2525

What to do.
What to say.

I cannot allow my sympathy to derail my plans. I cannot. 

And yet she has genuinely started to open up to me, and this makes it all the harder.


Have I ever gotten this close to a mark before? Not... not truly. I have had repeat encounters, but this- this is so near and dear to me, and every time she speaks to me she tells me things that only make her more admirable in my eyes. She tells me of her strength, and yet her kindness. Stars above.

I envy these people who love her, whom she loves. I envy the fact that she has managed to shake off the chains and conditioning of Arach. It tempts me, terribly, to take her up on her offer--and let her try to help me disappear.

I cannot do this. The Great Houses would find me. It would not work. But oh-- the temptation.

The bitter irony is, I could have loved *her*. Obviously this cannot be. But she is everything I would have admired. Clever. Cunning. Beautiful. Kind. 

How can I deprive someone of loving her when I myself would love her if the situation were different? And yet that is what I must do. I have to break all of their hearts for what--for a chance that I can continue to live without compromising my heart?

I have other options, of course.

I could tell her that I want this marriage to go through. This would not win her favor, though--our entire relationship's good will, on her end, is built on the fact that I do not want to force her to go through with this. It... it would be so easy to do this, though. Tell her that my matron has intimidated me, turned up the pressure on our marriage. Lie to her, tell her it's only for the politics, and... just hope that she comes to feel anything for me but bitterness.

I doubt she ever would. And so, I would once again be giving up my hope of love, even as I was married to the one I loved.

I could go clean at this point, of course. I could quietly shift my focus from betraying her to actually helping her, as I have claimed I am up to this point. She would never know that my motivations had changed. And... I could help her find happiness, without me. The Great Houses would almost undoubtedly catch us. But... this is the loving thing to do for her.

There are no good choices.
I can betray her to the Great Houses and preserve my own hopes for the future, even as I crush my feelings now.
I can betray her and tell her I want the marriage to go through, and she will resent me.
I can help her, and in doing so, lose her (and likely my own freedom).

I must betray her. It is the least painful option to me, personally.

And what do we have but ourselves, in the end?

I will still dream of what could have been, though. How cruel, that she is so good.
